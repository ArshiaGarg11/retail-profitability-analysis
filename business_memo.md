# Business Memo — Retail Profitability & Discount Impact Analysis

**To:** Chief Revenue Officer, Superstore Inc.

**From:** Business Analyst

**Date:** June 2026

**Re:** Profitability Analysis & Discount Strategy Review

## Overview

After going through 9,994 orders spanning 4 years, a few patterns stood out that are worth addressing. The overall profit margin sits at 12.47%, which isn't bad on the surface, but two issues are quietly eating into it — certain product lines that are actively losing money, and a discounting pattern that's getting out of hand in some regions.

## What the Data Shows

### Furniture Tables and Bookcases are not profitable
Tables brought in $207K in revenue but ended up with a net loss of $17,725 — that's a -8.56% margin. Bookcases aren't far behind at -3.02%. The main reason is discounting. Tables are being discounted at 26% on average, which is almost double the company average. Combined, these two sub-categories are wiping out around $21,197 in profit that should otherwise exist.

### The Central region is discounting too aggressively
Central is averaging a 24% discount rate, compared to 10.93% in the West. The impact is visible — Central's profit margin is 7.92% while the West sits at 14.94%. For context, Central is generating $501K in revenue but only $39.7K in actual profit. That's a lot of sales activity for very little return.

### High discounts and profitability don't go together — at all
Orders with no discount average a 29.51% profit margin. Once discounts cross 20%, margins flip negative at -15.3%. At 40%+, the business is losing 77 cents on every dollar of revenue. There's no instance in the data where heavy discounting led to a good outcome.

## Recommendations

**1. Put a cap on discounts at 20%**
Anything above 20% should need explicit approval. Based on the data, there are 1,393 orders that exceeded this threshold — fixing this going forward could recover around $135K in annual profit.

**2. Take a closer look at the Furniture Tables line**
Either the pricing needs to go up, the cost structure needs to come down, or the product needs to be reconsidered. Continuing to sell it at current margins doesn't make business sense.

**3. Investigate what's happening in the Central region**
The discount gap between Central and other regions is too large to be coincidental. It's worth understanding whether this is a sales culture issue, a competitive pressure issue, or something else — and then adjusting incentives so reps are rewarded on margin, not just revenue.

## Bottom Line

The revenue number — $2.3M annually — is solid. The problem is that too much of it is being given away through discounts before it ever becomes profit. None of these fixes require major investment, they mostly require tightening up existing practices.
